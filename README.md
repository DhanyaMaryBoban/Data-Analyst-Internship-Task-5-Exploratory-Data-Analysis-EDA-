# Data-Analyst-Internship-Task-5-Exploratory-Data-Analysis-EDA

**Objective**
- Extract insights using visual and statistical exploration. Tools: Python (Pandas, Matplotlib, Seaborn)

**Deliverables**
  - Jupyter Notebook + PDF report of findings

**Key Analysis Questions, Observations & Insights**

- **Which author published the most books?**
   	- **Observation:** Stephen King is the most published author, followed by John Grisham, Jodi Picoult, Charlaine Harris, Jim Butcher, and Nicholas Sparks.
    - **Insight:** Prolific authors gain visibility and reader loyalty. Publishing frequently helps sustain relevance.
    - **Suggestion**: Encourage consistent publishing strategies for authors to maintain market presence.
  
- **What are the most common genres?**
   - **Observation:** Genre fiction dominates (612 books), followed by nonfiction, fiction, and children’s books.
   - **Insight:** Genre fiction attracts wide audiences, while nonfiction and children’s books serve niche but stable markets.
   - **Suggestion:** Focus marketing on genre fiction, but diversify with nonfiction and children’s books.

- **Which language dominates the dataset?**
   - **Observation:** English-language books (eng + en-US) dominate, with other variants much less frequent.
   - **Insight:** English dominance reflects global publishing bias, limiting diversity.
   - **Suggestion:** Expand into non-English markets to reach new readers.

- **Strongest correlations among numerical features**
   - **Observation:** Units sold and sales rank are positively correlated. Gross sales and publisher revenue are positively correlated.
  - **Insight**: Higher unit sales improve rank visibility; gross sales directly drive publisher revenue.
  - **Suggestion**: Use promotions to boost unit sales and improve rankings.

- **Distribution of units sold**
   - **Observation**: Over 350 books sold fewer than 10,000 copies; fewer than 50 sold between 30,000–60,000. Distribution is right-skewed.
   - **Insight:** A “long-tail” market exists — most books sell modestly, while a few blockbusters dominate revenue.
   - **Suggestion:** Identify potential bestsellers early and allocate more marketing resources.

- **Genres with highest variability in gross sales**
   - **Observation:** Genre fiction shows high variability; nonfiction and fiction moderate; children’s books stable.
   - **Insight:** Genre fiction is risky but high-reward, while children’s books are predictable.
   - **Suggestion:** Balance portfolio — invest in children’s for stability, genre fiction for growth opportunities.

-  **Trend between book average rating and gross sales**
     - **Observation:** Most books with ratings 3.0–4.75 sell below 10,000 copies. A few mid-rated books (3.25–4.0) sell 20,000–50,000.
     - **Insight:** Ratings don’t strongly drive sales; marketing, author reputation, and demand matter more.
     - **Suggestion:** Focus on visibility and branding rather than relying only on ratings.
